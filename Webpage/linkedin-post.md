# LinkedIn Post — CompTIA Security+ Study Guide

I finished it. After months of work, my CompTIA Security+ (SY0-701) study guide is live and free for anyone to use.

https://securityplus-studyguide.vercel.app/

Here's what it actually is. The guide is organized around the five exam domains, weighted the way CompTIA weights them, so you spend the most time on the topics worth the most points. Each chapter pairs plain-language notes with original interactive diagrams I built to make the harder concepts click: things like the OSI model, the full TLS and IPsec handshakes, PKI and certificate chains, and the secure-versus-insecure port pairs you have to know cold. There's a master port reference and a bank of practice questions too. No clutter, no paywall, no account to create.

The content is built on the material from the CompTIA Security+ Get Certified Get Ahead SYO-701 Study Guide by Darril Gibson and Joe Shelley (Certification Experts LLC, 2023). I took what I was learning from that book and reorganized it into something interactive that I'd actually want to study from.

The part I care about most: share it. If you're trying to get into cybersecurity, the cost of resources should never be the thing that stops you. Use the guide, send it to a friend who's studying, drop it in your study group. That's what it's for.

I'm also using it myself. I'm sitting for my Security+ exam in early August, and building this has been the best preparation I could have asked for. You learn a topic differently when you have to explain it clearly to someone else.

If you're studying for Security+ or just figuring out where to start in security, keep going. The work adds up, the concepts connect, and the field has room for people who stay curious. I'm rooting for your success as much as my own.

Take a look, and let me know if it helps.
